Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice

nigerian jollof is a vibrant,smoky, and spicy one-pot rice dish that is a cornerstone of West African cuisine

INGREDIENTS

  • Rice
  • Tomato base
  • spices
  • Stock and Fat
  • groundnut oil

STEPS

  1. Prepare the Blend: Blend the red bell peppers, tomatoes, scotch bonnets, and one onion until smooth.
  2. Parboil the Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly, then parboil in boiling water for 5-10 minutes until slightly softened but not cooked. Wash with cold water to remove starch and set aside.
  3. Fry the Sauce: Heat oil in a large pot. Sauté the remaining sliced onion. Add tomato paste and fry for 5-10 minutes until it darkens and oil separates.
  4. Add Blend and Season: Pour in the blended pepper mix and fry for 10-15 minutes on medium heat to remove raw taste. Stir in curry, thyme, white pepper, bay leaves, garlic/ginger, bouillon cubes, and salt to taste.
  5. Add Stock and Rice: Add chicken/beef stock to the stew and bring to a boil. Pour in the parboiled rice and stir until the sauce coats every grain.
  6. Ensure Proper Water Level: The liquid should sit just slightly above the rice level. Add more water if necessary.
  7. Steam and Cook: Cover the pot with foil or parchment paper to lock in steam, then cover with a tight lid. Reduce heat to the lowest setting and cook for 30-40 minutes until the rice is tender and the water has evaporated.
  8. Final Steps: Remove from heat, stir gently, and let it rest. Optionally, increase heat for the last 2 minutes to create a smoky, scorched base.
